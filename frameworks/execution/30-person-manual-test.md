# 30-Person Manual Test

> "Before you buy any tool, don't even think about tools... Can you manually find 30 people that you want to spend 15 to 20 minutes writing a rock solid note to? Are there 30 people that you are deeply excited to learn from, that you are willing to invest 15 to 20 minutes to write a really thoughtful note?" - Jen Abel

## What It Is

The 30-Person Manual Test is a deliberate pre-automation exercise where founders manually research, write, and send highly personalized outreach to exactly 30 prospective customers. The goal is not volumeâ€”it's learning.

This approach prevents the common founder mistake of immediately setting up Clay, Apollo, or other automation tools and blasting thousands of prospects before understanding who they're actually trying to reach.

## How It Works

### The Exercise

1. **Find 30 people manually**
   - Use LinkedIn, industry publications, company websites
   - Choose people you're genuinely excited to learn from
   - If finding 30 is hard, that's a signal

2. **Spend 15-20 minutes per outreach**
   - Write each note specifically for that person
   - Use the RCCP framework (Relevancy, Counterintuitive, Concise, Problem-focused)
   - No templates, no automation

3. **Multi-channel approach**
   - Email
   - LinkedIn
   - Cold call (often highest response rate)
   - Twitter DM if relevant

4. **Track and analyze**
   - How many respond?
   - What do they say?
   - What patterns emerge?

### What You Learn

**From finding the 30:**
- Are they even discoverable?
- What commonalities exist (team size, industry, tenure, previous roles)?
- What parameters could you use for future targeting?

**From the responses (or lack thereof):**
- Does your message resonate?
- Is the problem felt?
- Is this the right role/persona?

**From conversations:**
- Is the problem real?
- How do they describe it?
- What would they pay for a solution?

### The Math Problem

If 0 out of 30 respond:
- Is it the message?
- Is it the audience?
- Is it the problem you're solving?

These questions are impossible to answer at scale. You need the manual learning first.

## How to Apply It

1. **Resist the tool temptation**
   - Engineers want to automate everything
   - Sales tools create false confidence
   - Blasting 5,000 emails teaches you nothing

2. **Make it painful on purpose**
   - The 15-20 minutes forces quality
   - You can't fake thoughtfulness at this pace
   - Each rejection or non-response is data

3. **Look for commonalities**
   - Same industry? Same company size?
   - Same stage of company? Same problem urgency?
   - This builds your targeting parameters

4. **Iterate before scaling**
   - Do another 30 with refined message
   - Do another 30 with different persona
   - Only automate when you understand the pattern

5. **Use all channels**
   - Don't rely on just email
   - Cold calling has surprisingly high response rates
   - Multi-touch increases connection probability

## When to Use It

- **Before buying any sales tools**: Clay, Apollo, Outreach, etc.
- **When entering a new market segment**: Test before scaling
- **When response rates drop**: Return to manual to diagnose
- **When pivoting your positioning**: Validate before automating

## The Discoverability Test

One often-overlooked benefit: this exercise tests whether your target market is even findable.

- Can you find 30 people who fit your criteria?
- If not, you have a fundamental go-to-market problem
- Some parameters are "undiscoverable" (net worth, intent signals)
- If you can't find them, you can't scale outreach to them

## Common Mistakes

- **Automating before understanding**: The biggest mistake
- **Doing 5 instead of 30**: Too small a sample
- **Rushing the outreach**: Quality over speed
- **Not multi-channeling**: Email alone isn't enough
- **Not analyzing patterns**: The learning is the point
